# Completion criteria by deliverable

Use this as the menu for the "Done when" line of the execution contract. Find the section that matches what you are producing and treat its points as the checklist you verify against before calling the work finished. These are defaults, not a cage: drop points that do not apply and add ones the specific task demands. The aim is a finish line you can actually observe, not a ritual.

## Document

- All requested sections are present.
- The narrative is coherent and reads in order.
- Unsupported claims are removed or sourced.
- The tone suits the audience and the seniority of the reader.
- The file exists in the requested format.
- The finished document has been read back for duplication and contradiction.

## Presentation

- The intended audience and the decision the deck is driving are clear.
- The storyline has a beginning, a middle, and a conclusion, not just a pile of slides.
- Each slide has one distinct job.
- Major claims carry evidence.
- The slide count fits the time available to present it.
- The actual presentation file has been created and reviewed, not just outlined.

## Research

- Credible sources have been examined, not just the first result.
- Fact and interpretation are kept distinct.
- Meaningful alternatives are compared, not a single option defended.
- Gaps and uncertainty are named rather than hidden.
- The result ends in a clear conclusion or recommendation.

## Product work (brief, PRD, spec)

- The problem and the target user are defined.
- The desired outcomes are stated.
- Scope and non-scope are both explicit.
- Requirements and acceptance criteria are usable by whoever builds it.
- Dependencies and risks are identified.
- Open questions are isolated and visible, not buried inside the prose.

## Spreadsheet or analysis

- The source data has been inspected before any calculation.
- Calculations are internally consistent and the totals reconcile.
- Assumptions are written down where a reader can see them.
- The requested outputs and any visualisations exist.
- Obvious anomalies have been checked rather than passed through.
- The sheet remains understandable and editable by a human, not a black box.

## File organisation

- The target structure exists.
- Files are placed by a coherent, stated rule.
- Nothing has been destroyed or overwritten without explicit permission.
- Duplicates and uncertain items are reported rather than silently resolved.
- The resulting structure is explained so the user can navigate it.

Note: organising is safe work, but deleting is not. Moving, renaming, and foldering can proceed. Removing "old versions", emptying anything, or overwriting an original crosses into destructive territory and needs explicit permission first. When in doubt, stage the deletions as a list and ask.

## Actions through connectors

- The correct records, messages, meetings, or files have been identified, not a near-match.
- The requested action has been performed.
- Anything destructive or externally visible (sending, publishing, deleting, paying, changing permissions) was authorised before it happened.
- The outcome has been checked where the connector allows it — the record now shows the change, the message appears in the thread, the event is on the calendar.
